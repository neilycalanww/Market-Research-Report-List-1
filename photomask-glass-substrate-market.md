<p><p>The photomask glass substrate market comprises primarily two types: quartz substrates and soda-lime substrates. Quartz substrates are valued for their superior optical clarity and high thermal stability, making them ideal for advanced photolithography applications in semiconductor manufacturing. In contrast, soda-lime substrates are more cost-effective and widely used for less demanding applications, offering good performance at a lower price point. Both substrates play critical roles in fabricating photomasks, which are essential for producing intricate circuit patterns in various electronic devices.</p></p>
