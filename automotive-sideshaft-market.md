<p><p>The automotive sideshaft market is primarily divided into two types: hollow sideshafts and rigid sideshafts. Hollow sideshafts are lightweight and designed to improve fuel efficiency while providing enhanced torque transmission. They are typically used in modern vehicles for their structural advantages. Rigid sideshafts, on the other hand, are characterized by their solid construction, offering increased durability and a higher load-bearing capacity. Both types play crucial roles in transferring engine power to the wheels, affecting vehicle performance and handling.</p></p>
