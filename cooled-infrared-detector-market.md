<p><p>The cooled infrared detector market features several key types. Photoconductive detectors, such as HgCdTe, are sensitive to infrared radiation and are used in high-performance applications. Photovoltaic detectors generate a voltage in response to absorbed photons, offering high sensitivity. Schottky barrier detectors utilize metal-semiconductor junctions for rapid response and low noise. Quantum well detectors leverage quantum mechanical principles for enhanced responsiveness. Other detectors may include those using novel materials or designs, catering to specific applications in surveillance, medical imaging, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
