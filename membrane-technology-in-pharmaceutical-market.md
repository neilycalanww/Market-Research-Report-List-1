<p><p>Membrane technology in the pharmaceutical market encompasses various filtration methods, each serving specific functions. Microfiltration is used for separating large particles and bacteria, while ultrafiltration targets smaller solutes and proteins. Nanofiltration typically removes multivalent ions and smaller organic molecules, and reverse osmosis effectively eliminates salts and small contaminants. Additionally, other specialized membrane processes like pervaporation and gas separation cater to unique applications. Together, these technologies enhance purification, concentration, and separation processes critical in pharmaceutical production.</p></p>
